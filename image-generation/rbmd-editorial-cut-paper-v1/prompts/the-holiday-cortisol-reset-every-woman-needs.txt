We talk a lot about abdominal weight gain, but rarely about the hormone driving it. Every year, people gain more than half of their annual weight between Thanksgiving and New Year’s and never lose it ( New England Journal of Medicine , 2000). We blame the cookies and cocktails, but the root cause of holiday weight gain often runs deeper: cortisol. Cortisol itself isn’t bad. You need it: it helps you wake with energy, think clearly, raise heart rate when needed, and maintain blood pressure. It’s adaptive and protective.
