VO₂ max—the maximum amount of oxygen your body can use during exercise—is the gold standard for cardiorespiratory fitness , and one of the strongest predictors of how long (and well) you’ll live. Boosting your VO₂ max by just 3.5 mL/kg/min slashes your risk of premature death by 11–17% ( British Journal of Sports Medicine , 2024).
